Rebirth of Freedom

About

The rebirth of Filipino literature from 1946 to 1970 followed the country's independence, highlighting an increase in newspapers and the return of writers. It lists significant literary works from this period, including notable poems, novels, and awards that inspired Filipino authors. The emergence of Tagalog literature was also noted alongside the establishment of the Palanca Memorial Awards for Literature in 1950.

Historical Background

The Americans returned in 1945. Filipinos rejoiced and guerrillas who fled to the mountains joined the liberating American Army. On July 4, 1946, the Philippines regained its freedom and the Filipino flag waved joyously alone. The chains were broken.

Novels and Short Stories in English

Writers began producing longer pieces. Stevan Javellana’s Without Seeing the Dawn depicts the grim war experiences under Japanese occupation.

In 1946, the PEN Center of the Philippines was inaugurated. Francisco Arcellana published his PEN Anthology of Short Stories. Later, Kerima Polotan’s The Hand of the Enemy (1961) and Luis V. Teodoro Jr.’s The Adversary (1968) gained recognition.

The New Filipino Literature

During the Japanese occupation, literature in Tagalog revived. Newspapers and magazines like Bulaklak, Liwayway, Ilang-Ilang, and Sinagtala reopened, giving hope and meaning through writing.

Five Selected Books:
  • Mga Piling Katha (1947–48) by Alejandro Abadilla
  • Ang Maikling Kuwentong Tagalog (1886–1948) by Teodoro Agoncillo

The Palanca Awards

Established: 1950
Founder: Carlos Palanca Sr.
Known as the “Pulitzer Prize of the Philippines,” it inspires Filipino writers to create excellent works in both English and Filipino.

Significance:
  • Encourages nationalism and creativity.
  • Promotes Philippine literature locally and internationally.
  • Continues to honor writers annually.
First Palanca Awardees (1950–1951)
  • Kuwento ni Mabuti – Genoveva Edroza
  • Mabangis na Kamay, Maamong Kamay – Pedro S. Dandan
  • Planeta, Buwan at mga Bituin – Elpidio P. Kapulong
